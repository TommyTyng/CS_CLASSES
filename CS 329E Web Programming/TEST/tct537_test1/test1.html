Test 1 (29 Jun 2018)

A friend of yours is starting a travel agency and would like you to design a website for her new company World Wide Travel. Here are some images that you must use on the site. You may re-size the images if you want to. You may add other images if you want to.

  • Beach
  • Cruise
  • Mountains
  • Plane
  • Safari
  • Taj Mahal

Her company will make airline reservations to exotic places and make arrangements for cruises and safaris.

The site will be minimalistic but aesthetically compelling. You must have a 3 column layout with a header, footer, navigation bar, body, and the third column giving information on promotions and/or upcoming tours.

Makeup a name for your imaginary friend and put your name and your imaginary friend's name in the upper right hand corner of the header.

Please set the site to maintain a width of 800px and height of 1000px.

There must be a link to a Contact Us page. In this page there will be a form that users can send information regarding their interests. The form must have the following:

  • Name of the client
  • E-mail address
  • Phone Number
  • Excursions that the client can choose that he is interested in
    • Cruise in the Bahamas
    • Mozart Festival in Austria
    • Explore the Pyramids

    Set the action field to be "/", the method to be "get" and the target to be "blank_".

    You may take up to 3 hours to finish this website. Make sure it works in Firefox. Once you finish you will zip the HTML, CSS, and image files together. The zipped file will be called your_uteid_test1.zip. Upload that zipped file to the Canvas system. Do not make any changes to your files after submission.

    Validate HTML files using https://validator.w3.org/ and CSS files using https://jigsaw.w3.org/css-validator/. Valid HTML is worth 5 points and valid CSS is worth 5 points.

    You may take more time than 3 hours if you need to. There is a penalty for late submission - 5 points for every half an hour late. The penalty is in units of every half hour

    You may not talk about the test to any of your classmates or with any other person in the world or get help in any form from someone else. You may use any book or any resource on the internet.
